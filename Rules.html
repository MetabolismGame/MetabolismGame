THE METABOLISM GAME

THE METABOLISM GAME is an enjoyable way for biology students to learn the
basic pathways and principles of the cell's energy metabolism. Each player
represents a living cell faced with the problem of acquiring energy from
carbon compounds in a convienent, usable form -- the ATP molecule. Players
move tokens representing carbon atoms through various enzymatic reations,
generating ATP as they go.

Object

The object of the game is to generate as much ATP as possible. The
winner may be the first player to accumulate an agreed-upon number of ATP
molecules (40 is a reasonable goal). If you prefer, ther winner may be the
player with the most ATP at a fixed time period.

Materials

THE GAME BOARD. Shows the pathways and enzymes of glycolysis (color-coded
blue) and the Krebs cycle (color-coded yellow).
ENZYME CARDS. A deck of 70 cards, including three of each of 22 different
enzymes, and four wild enzyme cards.
OXYGEN CARDS. Eight.
PLAYING TOKENS. Four sets of 18 tokens which represent carbon atoms.
ATP CURRENCY. Twenty each of three denominations, representing one, three,
and ten ATP molecules.
NADH+H- TOKENS. Ten.
FADH2 TOKENS. Ten.
